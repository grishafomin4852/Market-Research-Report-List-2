<p><p>A planar heat pipe, also known as a flat heat pipe or planer loop heat pipe, is a two-dimensional heat transfer device that utilizes capillary action and phase change heat transfer principles to efficiently move heat from one location to another. It consists of a sealed copper or aluminum container with a wick structure inside, filled with a working fluid. When heat is applied to one end of the heat pipe, the working fluid evaporates and the vapor travels to the cooler end, where it condenses and releases the heat. The condensed liquid then travels back to the hot end through the capillary action of the wick structure, completing the heat transfer loop.</p><p>The planar heat pipe market is experiencing significant growth and is anticipated to continue this trend in the forecast period. <p><p>Planar heat pipe market is classified into two main types: standard heat pipes and high conductivity plates. Standard heat pipes are designed with a flat geometry and consist of a sealed enclosure containing a wick structure and working fluid. These heat pipes are commonly used for transferring heat between two surfaces. On the other hand, high conductivity plates are made of materials with exceptional thermal conductivity and are used to enhance heat transfer in various applications. Both types play a crucial role in conducting and managing heat effectively in different systems and industries.</p></p>
