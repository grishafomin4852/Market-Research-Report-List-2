<p><p>High Precision Resistors refer to electronic components used to control or limit the flow of electric current in a circuit with high accuracy and reliability. There are two main types of High Precision Resistors: Chip Resistors and Wirewound Resistors. Chip Resistors are compact, surface mount devices commonly used in modern electronics. They are built using a thin film or thick film technology. Wirewound Resistors, on the other hand, are cylindrical components that consist of a resistive wire wound around a ceramic or metallic core. They are known for their ability to handle high power and offer excellent stability and precision.</p></p>
