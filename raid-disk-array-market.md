<p><p>The RAID disk array market includes various types of RAID configurations. RAID0, known as striping, splits data across multiple disks to enhance performance but lacks fault tolerance. RAID1, or mirroring, duplicates data across disks for better reliability. RAID5 stripes data with parity across multiple drives to provide performance and fault tolerance. RAID6 has similar functionality to RAID5 but with dual parity for added fault tolerance. RAID10 combines mirroring and striping for improved performance and fault tolerance. RAID50 utilizes a combination of RAID5 and RAID0 by striping across multiple RAID5 arrays. Other market options may include variations or combinations of these RAID types.</p></p>
