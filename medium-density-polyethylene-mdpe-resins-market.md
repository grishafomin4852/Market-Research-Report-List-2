<p><p>Medium Density Polyethylene (MDPE) resins are widely used in various industries due to their superior properties, such as excellent impact resistance, flexibility, and chemical resistance. Two common manufacturing processes for MDPE resins are the autoclave process and the tubular process. The autoclave process involves polymerization of ethylene under high pressure and temperature conditions, resulting in a resin with a narrow molecular weight distribution. On the other hand, the tubular process utilizes a continuous extrusion process to produce MDPE resins with a wider molecular weight distribution, enabling better processability and improved mechanical properties.</p></p>
