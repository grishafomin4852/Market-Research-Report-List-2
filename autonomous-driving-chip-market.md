<p><p>The autonomous driving chip market is segmented into different levels, namely L1, L2, L3, L4, and L5. L1 represents the most basic level, where the driver retains full control but can receive assistance from features like adaptive cruise control. L2 refers to partial automation, with advanced driver assistance systems (ADAS) that can control steering and acceleration but require driver oversight. L3 indicates conditional automation, where the car can handle most driving tasks but still relies on drivers for intervention. L4 represents high automation, where the car can operate without driver assistance in certain conditions. Finally, L5 represents full automation, where the car can handle all driving tasks in any condition.</p></p>
