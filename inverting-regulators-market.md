<p><p>In the inverting regulators market, there are two main types: Integrated Switch and External Switch. Integrated Switch regulators incorporate an internal switch that enables the regulation of the input voltage to produce a negative output voltage. On the other hand, External Switch regulators require an external switch to perform the voltage inversion operation. Integrated Switch regulators are typically more compact and cost-effective, while External Switch regulators provide greater flexibility and customization options. Both types cater to different application needs and have their own advantages and disadvantages.</p></p>
