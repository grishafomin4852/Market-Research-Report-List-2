<p><p>The Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) Market is categorized into three types based on the level of invasiveness. Invasive BCI involves surgical implantation of electrodes directly into the brain, offering high accuracy but posing higher risks. Non-invasive BCI utilizes external devices such as EEG headsets placed on the scalp but offers lower precision. Partially invasive BCI combines elements of both by placing electrodes on the outer layer of the brain, providing higher accuracy than non-invasive methods while minimizing risks associated with invasive techniques. These types cater to different needs and applications in the BCI industry.</p></p>
